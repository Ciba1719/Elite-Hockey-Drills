Warm-up / Mobility

Down Dog to Cobra Flow

Hamstringscalveship flexorsspine Aerobic
i.

How to Do It

From a push-up position, push your hips up and back into a downward dog, feeling the hamstrings and calves. Then flow forward, drop the hips, and lift the chest into a cobra, opening the front of the body. Move back and forth slowly, breathing with each transition.

ii.

Why It Works

One continuous flow that lengthens the whole posterior chain, then the whole anterior chain, with the breath.

iii.

Hockey Transfer

A quick, whole-body mobility reset, ideal on off days to keep the joints feeling fresh.

iv.

Coaching Cues

  • "Move with the breath, slow and controlled."
v.

Common Mistakes

Rushing the transitions; locking the knees hard in down dog; cranking the lower back in cobra.

vi.

Progression / Regression

Progression

Add small range at the end of each position.

Regression

Bend the knees in down dog and keep the cobra shallow.
