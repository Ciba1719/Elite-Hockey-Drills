Warm-up / Mobility

Tibialis Raise

Tibialis anteriorankle Alactic / ATP-PC
i.

How to Do It

Stand with your heels about 15 cm from a wall and lean lightly back against it, heels staying on the floor. Lift your toes up toward your shins as high as possible, then lower slowly. You should feel a strong burn down the front of the shin.

ii.

Why It Works

It directly strengthens the tibialis anterior, the muscle that decelerates the foot and is usually neglected.

iii.

Hockey Transfer

Stronger shins mean better balance, cleaner stops and cuts, and protection against shin splints and ankle tweaks.

iv.

Coaching Cues

  • "Toes high. Slow on the way back down."
v.

Common Mistakes

Rushing the lowering; not getting full range; letting the heels lift.

vi.

Progression / Regression

Progression

Add load over the toes, or do it one leg at a time.

Regression

Reduce the reps or the lean angle.

vii.

Primary Muscles

Tibialis anteriorankle
viii.

Energy System
