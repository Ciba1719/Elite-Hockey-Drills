Upper Body / Push

Push-Up to Shoulder Tap

Chesttricepscore Alactic / ATP-PC
i.

How to Do It

Start in a push-up plank. Perform a push-up, then at the top tap the opposite shoulder with one hand without letting your hips rotate. Alternate the tapping hand each rep. The whole point is that the hips stay perfectly still while one hand leaves the floor.

ii.

Why It Works

Adding a shoulder tap to a push-up layers an anti-rotation core demand on top of pushing strength.

iii.

Hockey Transfer

Builds the rigid trunk and pushing strength you need to hold ground in puck battles and contact.

iv.

Coaching Cues

  • "Stay square. Hips don't move."
v.

Common Mistakes

Hips rotating when the hand lifts; rushing the taps; a sagging plank.

vi.

Progression / Regression

Progression

Widen the feet less (a narrower base is harder), or slow the tempo.

Regression

Drop to the knees, or remove the push-up and just tap.
