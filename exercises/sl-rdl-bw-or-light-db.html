Hinge / Posterior Chain

SL RDL (BW or Light DB)

Hamstringsgluteship stabilizerscore Strength/neural

Demo video coming soon

i.

How to Do It

Standing on one leg with a soft knee, hold light or no weight. Hinge from the hip, extending the free leg behind and lowering the torso until roughly parallel to the floor, body forming a straight line from head to heel. Keep the back flat and hips level, then return to standing. Move slowly with control. Switch sides.

ii.

Why It Works

Grooves the single-leg hinge pattern and balance with minimal load, developing hamstring/glute control, proprioception, and the pelvic stability to keep the hips square — building a foundation before loading.

iii.

Hockey Transfer

Reinforces the balance and single-leg hip control demanded by gliding on one edge; improves stride stability and proprioception that supports tight turns and recovering balance after contact.

iv.

Coaching Cues

  • "Body in one line"
  • "hips level, square to the floor"
  • "slow and controlled"
v.

Common Mistakes

Hips opening to the side; bending the standing knee too much (squatting); rushing/losing balance

vi.

Progression / Regression

Progression

add a light DB or close eyes

Regression

lightly tap the toe down or hold a support
